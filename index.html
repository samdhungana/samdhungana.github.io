Hi, I'm Sam.

I'm a rising senior at Harvard studying Applied Mathematics with an Economics honors track. This site is a place to keep the things I actually spend my time on in one spot: research, the outdoors, and whatever I'm building next.

I work as a research assistant in Harvard's Economics Department, where I clean and model real datasets on interstate conflict and household migration for faculty research. Last summer I was a fiscal policy intern for the Government of Gandaki Province in Nepal, writing revenue recommendations that senior officials weighed against real budget constraints. More recently I've been spending time on independent equity research, building full valuation models on individual companies out of genuine interest in how businesses actually work.

Outside of that, I lead trips for the Harvard Outing Club into the White Mountains, serve as a College Director on the Harvard Alumni Association Board, and treasure being outdoors more than being anywhere else. I also like to take photos. I've photographed major events for CS50 and Phillips Brooks House Association.
